History of Harare

Harare, founded in 1890 and originally known as Salisbury, is Zimbabwe’s capital city and the heart of business, culture, and government. It became "Harare" in 1982, named after the Shona chief Neharawa. Today, the city blends colonial-era architecture, modern development, and a vibrant arts and music scene.

Current Demographics

  • Population: ~1.6 million (metropolitan)
  • Main Languages: Shona, English, Ndebele
  • Economy: Finance, retail, agriculture, manufacturing, technology
  • Median Age: ~20 years
